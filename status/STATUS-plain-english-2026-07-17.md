# Where the framework stands, in plain English

*2026-07-17. Written after the Tier-2 go/no-go (phase 38) and the full expert-audit sweep (phases 39–42). Everything here is a translation of the tagged record; the phase documents remain the authority.*

## What the framework is claiming, in one paragraph

The world is fundamentally a three-dimensional space with a layered, "folded" geometric structure. Time is not fundamental: it emerges as the natural internal flow that this folded structure generates. The same fold that generates the clock also forces the world to be quantum — probabilistic rather than deterministic. Gravity, in this picture, should emerge as the thermodynamics of that structure.

## The claims that survive (and have now been audited)

**1. If the world's basic yes/no questions are the ones this geometry provides, quantum probability is forced.** Not "consistent with" — forced: the Born rule is the *only* way to assign probabilities to those questions, deterministic hidden values are mathematically impossible, and every physical state is irreducibly statistical and thermal. This chain is built from decades-old theorems, and the audit verified each one against the original papers. It is the program's most solid possession.

**2. The fold makes both the dice and the clock.** The single geometric feature at the heart of the framework (a nonzero Godbillon–Vey invariant) simultaneously forces the statistical character above *and* activates an intrinsic time flow. Two-thirds of the "stiffness is the clock" claim is now theorem-level (verified word-for-word against Connes); the remaining third is a single missing conversion factor — one scale.

**3. Locally, the framework is exactly standard physics.** The fold has no local density, so no tabletop or beamline experiment can see it. This is why the framework passes every precision test automatically — and, honestly, why it has no local new effects to offer.

**4. Where the framework's clock can be checked against known physics, it agrees exactly.** The Unruh temperature comes out right, with the required technical hypotheses now honestly listed, and the clock-temperature relation reproduces it with no freedom.

**5. The desk mathematics keeps passing.** The sector/statistics structure survived its tests and now makes an exact, falsifiable table of predictions (which flux sectors are bosonic, which fermionic, in what numbers), checkable by a Floer theorist at a desk.

## What died, honestly

**The triple-slit hope is dead.** The framework predicts exactly zero anomalous three-path interference — as a theorem, not a disappointment to be hidden. Every route to making it nonzero and measurable was closed, one by one. There is no lab-scale fingerprint.

**The numerical "predictions" of the adjacent AMK program (cosmological constant, Higgs mass, neutrino masses) are fitting, not physics.** The audit found the claimed shared modulus never actually enters any of their formulas, the functional forms change from paper to paper, and the authors admit in print to scanning an infinite family until a number fits. We do not cite those numbers, ever. (The structural ideas we borrowed from them survive; the numerology does not.)

## The unknowns that remain — there are exactly four

1. **The join (the big one).** Everything above says "the geometry's algebra behaves quantum-mechanically and carries a clock." Whether the *real world's* observables are that algebra is the central unproven identification. Every conditional claim routes through it.
2. **The one scale.** The framework's raw material is dimensionless. It needs exactly one conversion to the real world's units — and it now makes a sharp claim: fix any one of (Newton's constant, Planck's constant, the second) and the other two follow. The missing piece is one identification ("a tick of the clock = the light-crossing time of one fold") plus one hard computation (the entropy density of the folded vacuum).
3. **One clean math question.** Is the relevant operator algebra a factor, of the finest type (III₁)? Assumed throughout, established nowhere. A specialist could settle it.
4. **Is the fold *the* reason for time, or just *a* reason?** The fold is sufficient to activate intrinsic time but provably not necessary. That it is the physically operative one is the framework's own bet.

## What we can honestly say the framework predicts

**One sharp prediction under active fire right now:** dark energy does not evolve. w = −1 exactly, at all times, with no parameter to adjust — because the underlying geometry is rigid. Current data (DESI) actually *leans against* this at the ~3–4σ level, so this is a real, live, dangerous prediction. Euclid's first data release late this year is the referee. If evolution is confirmed and smooth, the cosmological channel dies — and we have pre-registered that only step-like evolution could save a geometric reading. Companion prediction: dark matter is exactly pressureless, at every epoch.

**A family of exact nulls the framework is committed to and cannot walk back:** gravitational waves travel exactly as general relativity says (no damping, no dispersion, no birefringence, exactly two polarizations); no triple-slit anomaly at any sensitivity, ever; no Unruh/Hawking temperature anomaly; no non-gravitational dark matter detection, ever. These are shared with standard physics — they are not fingerprints — but each is a genuine kill switch: one confirmed anomaly in any of them and the framework is dead.

**An exact mathematical census, checkable at a desk:** the statistics table — per universe and per flux sector, precisely how many protected bosonic and fermionic states exist (e.g., maximal-flux sectors purely bosonic; one fermion pair per handle in the next sector; dominance alternating down the ladder).

**The potential crown jewel — not yet earned:** if the join and the one scale are ever established, the framework stops *measuring* Newton's constant and starts *computing* it (and the ¼ in black-hole entropy with it). That is the payoff the remaining theory work is aimed at. Until then it is an ambition, not a prediction.
