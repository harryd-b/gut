# Fresh red team — the five independent referee reports, verbatim (2026-07-18)

*Methodology: five referee agents were instantiated with NO access to this session's context, memory, or defenses — each received only its track's mailing (the packet cover + that track's exhibits, exactly as a real recipient would) and instructions to verify claims against the primary literature, recompute arithmetic independently, and report as the busy senior academic each simulates. Unlike the in-context rehearsals (phases 67, 70, 72, 73), these referees could not know what the record considers settled, could not be steered by the record's own framing, and did their own literature searches. Their reports are preserved verbatim below; the synthesis and damage report are in phase 74.*

## Report 1 — Track W (worldsheet/CFT theorist)

Ninety percent of unsolicited theory-of-everything mail dies at the first paragraph, and this one nearly did: the cover document is written in a hermetic in-house dialect ("the fold," "the carrier," "K-ST0," "B3c discipline," "the join") that presumes the reader has read seventy-odd "phases" of an internal diary. Two things made me read on: the Track W paragraph name-drops the correct, current literature — not the usual crank canon — and the packet contains actual falsifiable arithmetic with an enclosed script, plus a document (phase 55) that voluntarily concedes 60–70% of its machinery is rediscovery.

**Claims checked (all recomputed independently):** the minimal-string anomaly relations (b² = p/p′, the 26 and 15 sums, ghost charges) — all verified exactly, textbook material correctly transcribed. The Diophantine menus at g = 2 — arithmetic verified. The super-minimal counting formulas and the "empty by parity" theorem — exactly right, independently recounted from the Kac table; also two lines of elementary parity that evaporates under component counting, as the authors admit. TIM = M(4,5) = SM(3,5) — correct. The Belavin–Knizhnik chain — textbook-correct coefficient for coefficient; but the "exact bulk cancellation by isospectrality" presented as a discovery in phase 53 is the standard mechanism behind Quillen/BK factorization — rediscovery inside a document about rediscovery. The phase 40 census table — all 20 rows recomputed from Macdonald's generating function, every entry correct. The III₁ assembly — valid citations, and physically empty (every hyperfinite III₁ factor is isomorphic to every other; that is what Haagerup's theorem means), which their own §3 concedes while the cover re-inflates it.

**Errors/overstatements:** (1) "b is pinned... There is no scan" — false in spirit: the scan over b was replaced by a scan over identifications (bosonic-total, allocation-A, super-total, super-NS, ST-0b), which reach Ising, tricritical Ising, the empty set, or non-unitary junk at will. Two "mutually exclusive candidates, both flagged" is not a pinned menu. (2) The count-level identification contradicts the record's own finer data: phase 40 gives g=2 sector ranks (1, 6, 1); phase 69 matches 3 sectors to Ising's 3 primaries ignoring ranks; allocation A matches the rank 6 to M(4,5)'s primary count. Different species of integer, equated as convenient. No conformal weight, fusion rule, or modular datum matched anywhere. (3) K-ST0 is "theater with a theorem attached" — the kill fires only under four unproven identification choices, any of which would be abandoned if the answer landed there. (4) Phase 68's "two independent routes — one cone" is not independent: the Borchers relation and the sl(2,ℝ) bracket are the same Lie-algebra statement; the coincidence of null directions is the root-space decomposition, a tautology. (5) Genus two is never selected by anything — at g=3 the menu has no unitary matter at all; whether that is a prediction ("the universe is genus 2") or a reductio is nowhere discussed. (6) The mailing is not self-contained: the phases defining the census's physical meaning, the sum rule's derivation, and the anomaly books are cited and not enclosed.

**Genuinely interesting:** the hygiene is real (every recomputed integer correct, every checked citation real — "the failure mode here is not error but unmoored correctness"); phase 55 is "something I have never received from an independent program"; one well-posed question survives: does the parity-graded Muñoz–Wang data arise as twisted-sector content of some Γ-equivariant chiral system? — which their own Route B verdict correctly identifies as probably shallow.

**Asks:** L1-aud — unanswerable as posed (internal construction not enclosed). D-test-1 — not currently a defined computation. Γ-automorphy — the field owns adjacent problems; I may think about them for my reasons, not theirs.

**Verdict:** "This is the most scrupulously bookkept numerology I have ever been mailed. The arithmetic is flawless, the citations are real... and none of it adds up to a result, because the entire worldsheet content rests on equating small integers from a Floer-theoretic census with primary counts of minimal models... with enough convention freedom to hit Ising, tricritical Ising, or the empty set at will... until a conformal weight, a fusion rule, or one modular matrix element crosses from their census to a CFT and matches, there is nothing here for a worldsheet theorist to referee. Decline, with the above notes; do not encourage resubmission until D-test-1 is defined by them and run by them."

## Report 2 — Track O (operator algebraist)

**Triage:** read past the cover only because the lead claim is falsifiable from my chair. The cover's self-referential idiom is a wall; "a four-page mathematical note would have been read by more people, faster."

**Claims checked:** (a) W*(T¹Σ_g, F_ws) hyperfinite III₁ — **correct, and long-known, with one delicious irony: phase 43's corrections ledger states "there is no 'Bowen, Anosov foliations are hyperfinite' paper." False. R. Bowen, "Anosov foliations are hyperfinite," Ann. of Math. 106 (1977) 549–565, is real** (confirmed via Tondeur's foliations bibliography; Spatzier 1987, which they cite correctly, itself references it). The "correction ledger" introduced a bibliographic error while advertising its own rigor. The mathematical claim survives regardless. (b) Haagerup + BDF — citations correct (BDF needs nuclearity/split hypotheses; conclusion is R ⊗ Z), conclusion physically empty, which they half-admit and keep headlining. (c) The Borchers identification — the Lie-algebra coincidence is "real, standard, and thin"; the packet never mentions the Araki–Zsidó repair of Wiesbrock's converse, which a specialist would; R-aud1 is "not a months-of-work open problem, it is a two-paragraph argument via Araki–Zsidó" once restated for weights (as posed, the question isn't even well-formed — HSMI is defined relative to a vector, the carrier's natural object is a weight). The v3.1 downgrade is the correct epistemic position; but phase 68's bolded "light propagates along horocycles [derived]" survives in the exhibit and should not — the boundary net where BW genuinely holds is the unquotiented Möbius-covariant net; Γ-invariance destroys the covariance and the vacuum the derivation needs. (d) Phase 32/39 Gleason assembly — mathematically sound, the phase-39 correction is "a real specialist move," novelty thin and honestly stated (Rédei–Summers, Clifton–Halvorson territory). (e) Phase 45's caught error and repair — "the best thing in the packet," the textbook Takesaki continuous decomposition correctly instantiated. **But the normalization claim fails: the 2π in 𝒯 = 2πR/c belongs to interval algebras of Möbius-covariant nets with vacuum; the compact carrier has no vacuum state and no Möbius covariance; importing that 2π is an analogy presented as a derivation, and the downstream "Gibbons–Hawking form emerging rather than imposed" is circular once you see where the 2π came from.** (f) The EQ chain — every imported theorem checks out verbatim (Hurder–Katok Thm 3.11 verified against the IHÉS text; Katok rigidity; the higher-dim gate really is Katok's conjecture, still open); the assembly is correct known mathematics; EQ itself is one undischarged postulate, and "the framework thereby predicts the Katok conjecture matters for physics" predicts nothing.

**Asks:** J1 — **defined nowhere in the enclosed material**, and their own phase 43 demotes it; no. H6b — one-to-two weeks, not months, and its nontrivial regime is the regime the framework discards; a student warm-up. R-aud1 — "I would answer it, once, in an email," with the Bowen erratum attached.

**Verdict:** "By a wide margin the most citation-accurate piece of unsolicited theory-of-everything mail I have received... and it still does not contain a single new theorem in my field, because every mathematical statement in it is either decades-old known results reassembled, a postulate (EQ), an analogy promoted past its hypotheses (the 2π, the 'causal skeleton'), or a question (R-aud1) whose answer is an afternoon's work that the senders could not do and I can... honest scaffolding around an empty building is still an empty building. I will send the R-aud1 negative and the Bowen erratum as a courtesy; I decline the rest, and I would advise the human signatory to extract phases 45–47 as a short mathematics-only expository note and let the rest of the seventy phases go."

## Report 3 — Track F (Floer/gauge theorist)

**Triage:** past the cover barely — Track F leads with checkable statements with arXiv numbers, and the ledger openly retracts its own results, "not typical crackpot behavior."

**Claims checked:** (a) Muñoz–Wang attribution and the k ≠ 0 census — **correct** (Theorem 1.7 verified verbatim; the transport through HM ≅ HF⁺ legitimate; phase 48's honesty about the bridge "more careful than most published physics I referee"). (b) The Macdonald table and deficit column — **arithmetic exact** (all rows recomputed; closed forms g·4^{g−1} and (3g+1)·4^{g−2}, which they never state); **but "the deficit column stands as data" is false as written** — the k = 0 rows are pure extrapolation into the torsion sector where Theorem 1.7 explicitly does not apply; 60% of the flagship deficit entry is conjecture. (c) **The k = 0 "rank 6" at g = 2 is NOT a Floer-theoretic fact and is contradicted by their own cited source.** Jabuka–Mark (math/0502328): in the torsion sector HF⁺ is infinitely generated; HF⁺_red vanishes for g ≤ 2; ĤF total rank ≥ 8 > 6 at g = 2; 2-torsion appears for g > 2. **The g = 1 sanity check is fatal: the extrapolation predicts rank 1 for T³, while ĤF(T³) has rank 6.** The "6" is the Macdonald pattern continued into the one sector where it is known to break. Worse, phase 71 makes it load-bearing with the parenthetical "(= H*(Σ₂), Referee F confirmed)" — **"Referee F" is, per their own rehearsal records, a simulated referee. A role-played version of me 'confirmed' the one number that is wrong.** (d) OS parity purity and the Brieskorn table — **correct** (OS Cor 1.4 verified verbatim; phase 28 "genuinely competent work"). (e) **Which 3-manifold? The program's carrier is T¹Σ_g (Euler number 2g−2); the entire census is computed on the trivial bundle Σ_g×S¹. Different manifolds, different monopole Floer homology; the correct tool is Mrowka–Ozsváth–Yu (math/9612221), cited nowhere in the packet.** Phase 65 §4.3 visibly straddles the two and files the discrepancy as a pleasing observation rather than the red flag it is. (f) The parity clause — half-right: standard Witten-style folklore as an effective statement; overreach as a fundamental statistics clause (twisting decouples cohomological parity from spin-statistics).

**Asks:** ESCH — under-specified (rank bookkeeping under maps that do not preserve rank); properly posed it is a student-project-sized exercise whose answer adjudicates no cosmology. Jacobian-dressing calibration — "the literature has already answered more of it than they acknowledge — Jabuka–Mark IS the calibration, and it says towers + Λ*H¹ + torsion, not six slots." D-test-1's rank-6-into-six-slots — "dead on arrival" until flavor/coefficients are named, the ≥ 8 and g=1 contradictions reconciled, and the manifold decided. Would send one corrective email with Jabuka–Mark and MOY, "because the packet's stated culture suggests they would actually run the correction."

**Verdict:** "The strangest unsolicited mailing I have received: the literature citations are, without exception among the six I checked, verbatim-correct... which is exactly why the failure mode is instructive: the single number carrying the entire genus-two superstructure is the one number that is not a theorem, is contradicted by the very paper they cite for that sector, fails its own g = 1 sanity check, lives on a manifold that may be the wrong one relative to their own ontology, and was 'confirmed' only by a simulated referee wearing my job description... if they ever send a mailing where the load-bearing number has a theorem under it, I would read that one too."

## Report 4 — Track C (contact topologist)

**Triage:** past the cover only because Track C is short and its first paragraph names real theorems correctly, with the right corrections already made (projectively-Anosov vs Anosov; loose/non-loose; no torsion additivity; Gay vs Ghiggini). **Errors/overstatements:** the load-bearing slogan "tightness ⟺ the Bennequin bound exists ⟺ every knot's twisting has a maximum" is an overstatement dressed as a biconditional — tight ⟹ Bennequin is a theorem; the reverse reading is nonstandard and effectively circular; and tight manifolds need not contain knots saturating tb+|r| = −χ. "The geometry bounds its own matter content" is rhetoric beyond what C1–C6 support. Phase 68's "are the Borchers relations" is too strong (no positive-energy HSMI on the compact quotient — which they largely retract themselves).

**Asks:** M-aud3 — an afternoon, essentially already done; would confirm in one email. **GV-T — underspecified with a type mismatch: GV is real-valued, torsion is ℕ∪{∞}, "the Bennequin defect structure" is not a defined invariant; and the ET approximations are universally tight, so the "twist budget" may be close to empty on this class. Not engageable until someone pins one target invariant and states a conjectured inequality with both sides defined.** But the GV-T corner is real: Hurder–Katok and Mitsumatsu–Hozoori "genuinely have not been joined this way."

**Verdict:** "The contact-topology core is, surprisingly, not crank... But strip the AI apparatus and the net new mathematics is one correct-but-elementary observation, one trivial lemma, and one open question that is intriguing only as a slogan and unposed as a problem — all in the service of a sprawling edifice I have no reason to take seriously and every reason not to. A two-line reply confirming M-aud3, noting GV-T needs its target invariant, and declining the framework. Competent borrowing, honest bookkeeping, no result."
