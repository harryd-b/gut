## D. Bonus — partial progress on front #2 (non-SUSY "geometry → dynamics")

While here, the cheap lit-check on front #2 (does a non-SUSY special-geometry principle exist?) returned a real lead: **tt\* geometry** (Cecotti–Vafa) is exactly "the geometry of the vacuum bundle over theory space," a *far-reaching generalization of special geometry*, and it has been applied in **non-supersymmetric** settings (e.g. closed-string tachyon potentials). **So a candidate non-SUSY "geometry determines the effective action" principle exists.**

**Honest caveat (and it's the crux for the original bet):** in the known non-SUSY applications, tt\* geometry still **leans on a residual $(2,2)$/$\mathcal N=2$ worldsheet structure**. So the evidence is *mixed*: the geometric principle extends *past manifest spacetime SUSY*, but keeps reappearing **tethered to a $(2,2)$ algebraic structure**. Preliminary read on front #2: the answer is likely **"a geometric principle exists, but only with a residual $(2,2)$ scaffold"** — which would mean the original *fully* non-SUSY bet stays dead, while a *weaker* "needs only worldsheet/holomorphic $(2,2)$ structure, not spacetime SUSY" version might live. That is the sharpened front #2, and it's a genuine next research question (tt\* geometry without any $(2,2)$ structure — does geometry still fix the effective action?).
