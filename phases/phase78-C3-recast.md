## 1. C3′ — the recast constraint [registered; replaces every prior form of C3]

Any candidate dictionary (any boundary theory proposed to realize the framework's protected content) must reproduce, on the nose:

1. **The sector lattice:** superselection-type sectors labeled by ℤ/(2g−2) — the torsion classes of the carrier — not a ℤ-ladder, not 2g−1 of anything. [established carrier structure, phase 75]
2. **The universal part:** in *every* sector, a tower structure whose associated algebra is the free graded-commutative algebra modeled on Λ*H¹(Σ_g) — 2g odd generators — with the standardness of phase 75's Proposition (triple-cup vanishing) as its signature: **no sector-dependent exotic corrections**. A candidate whose generic sector content varies across sectors, or whose tower algebra is not an exterior algebra on exactly 2g odd generators, fails. [established, REMARK-1 Prop 1]
3. **The protected part:** exactly **one** distinguished class, in exactly the **spin-type sector** [g−1], at the grading law of REMARK-1 Prop 2 — and *nothing else*: every other sector's reduced/protected content must be **zero**. [established, REMARK-1 Prop 2]

C3′ is far more falsifying than the census it replaces — **with one honesty clause added 2026-07-18 (FR2-6), which is the constraint's true status: C3′ is, as written, a correct description of the Heegaard Floer package of one 3-manifold. It becomes a *boundary condition on a theory* only through a functor — from the candidate theory's data to spin-c-graded modules on T¹Σ_g — and that functor does not exist. Naming it: F-GAP. F-GAP is not a missing lemma of C3′; it IS the dictionary problem, and every sentence below that says "a candidate must show X" means "a candidate equipped with a proposed functor must show X."** Two further corrections from the same referee: the type/genus mismatch is real (a CFT's superselection structure is genus-independent; C3′'s data are genus-dependent — so the candidate object is a 3d (spin-)TQFT evaluated on the carrier, not a CFT's abstract sector category); and item 3's "spin-structure dependence" overstated the Floer input — what is selected is the self-conjugate *degree* (shared by all theta characteristics), with genuine theta-dependence being the conjectural sharpening of §3, not the theorem.
