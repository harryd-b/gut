## 1. The diagnosis: how the error happened

The census (phases 27/28/40) was built on Σ_g×S¹ via Muñoz–Wang. The carrier switch to T¹Σ_g = Γ\PSL(2,ℝ) happened in phase 43 — *after* the census was already load-bearing — and the census was never migrated. ## 2. The verified structure on the carrier [all sourced]

Let N = T¹Σ_g, the circle bundle of Euler number ±(2g−2) over Σ_g, g ≥ 2.

**(a) Only torsion sectors exist.** H²(N;ℤ) ≅ ℤ^{2g} ⊕ ℤ/(2g−2); spin-c structures pulled back from the base have torsion c₁, forming a torsor over ℤ/(2g−2). MOY (math/9612221, Thm 5.9.1, quoted): the SW moduli for E⊗W_c is non-empty **iff** E is a pullback — all solutions live in torsion sectors. Independently: any non-torsion c₁ pairs nontrivially with an embedded vertical torus, and the adjunction theorem (Ozsváth–Szabó math/0105202, Thm 1.6, with g(T) = 1) forces HF⁺ = 0 there. **The census ladder on the carrier is the finite ℤ/(2g−2) — exactly 2g−2 classes — not the ℤ-ladder of the trivial bundle.** [established (others'); the vanishing specialization is an elementary inference, flagged as such]

**(b) The moduli, sector by sector (MOY Cor. 1.0.5, quoted verbatim in the agent report).** The full SW moduli space of a nontrivial circle bundle of degree n over Σ_g is: **n copies of the Jacobian torus T^{2g} of reducibles — one per torsion class — plus irreducible components C±(e) ≅ Sym^e(Σ) for 0 ≤ e ≤ g−2**, with C⁺(e) in torsion class e and C⁻(e) (the K⊗E^{−1} partner) in class −e mod (2g−2). Consequences: the **irreducible-carrying classes number 2g−3** ({0, ±1, …, ±(g−2)} mod 2g−2), and the **middle class g−1 carries reducibles only** — it is also precisely the MOY-degenerate case (Cor. 5.8.5: Morse–Bott degeneracy exactly when E ≅ π*K^{1/2}, the spin-type sector). The carrier has its own "hard sector," and it is the spin one.

**(c) The block correspondence — the old census survives the move.** The old non-torsion sectors r = ±1, …, ±(g−1) carried H\*(Sym^{g−1−|r|}Σ); the carrier's irreducible families carry Sym^e(Σ), e = 0, …, g−2. Under e = g−1−|r| (sign ↔ family) this is a **bijection with identical symmetric-product blocks**: [computed, §block-correspondence in `fr2_census_calc.py`] every finite block of the old census reappears as a MOY component on the carrier, reorganized onto the torsion ladder — with collisions (C⁺(e) and C⁻(e) share class 0 at e = 0; generally classes fold) and with the reducible Jacobian added to every class. **The old k = 0 sector — the one Jabuka–Mark showed infinitely generated with nonstandard HF^∞ — has no counterpart: it was an artifact of the trivial bundle.** Its structural role (the "hard sector") passes to the middle class g−1.

**(d) The carrier is cleaner than the trivial bundle — a new, easy, apparently unrecorded fact.** For n ≠ 0, H¹(N) = π*H¹(Σ) and cup products of pulled-back one-classes land in the torsion ℤ/n, so **the integral triple cup product form of T¹Σ_g vanishes identically** — hence by Lidman (arXiv:1011.4277, Thm 1.1: HF^∞ for torsion spin-c is cup homology) **HF^∞(T¹Σ_g, s; 𝔽₂) ≅ Λ*(𝔽₂^{2g}) ⊗ 𝔽₂[U,U⁻¹] is STANDARD in every sector, despite b₁ = 2g ≥ 4** — in sharp contrast to Σ_g×S¹, where the triple cup form is nonzero and HF^∞ is nonstandard with 2-torsion (Jabuka–Mark Thm 1.2, Cor 1.3). [derived, elementary, from Lidman + Gysin; the literature agent found it stated nowhere — a candidate one-page remark, and exactly the kind of "load-bearing number with a theorem under it" the fresh Track F referee asked to see]

**(e) The finite census exists and is already in print — up to one retrieval.** For torsion spin-c, HF⁺ = towers ⊕ finite HF_red; the canonical finite census on the carrier is **HF_red(T¹Σ_g, s) per torsion class**, and Ozsváth–Szabó computed the reduced Heegaard Floer homology of **every nontrivial circle bundle over a Riemann surface** (𝔽₂ coefficients) in "Knot Floer homology and integer surgeries," AGT 8 (2008) 101–154, **Theorem 5.6** — with no restriction excluding the borderline degree 2g−2. The ECH mirror: Nelson–Weiler (arXiv:2007.13883, Thm 1.1) compute ECH of exactly these prequantization bundles per torsion class Γ ∈ {0,…,2g−3} — nonzero in **all 2g−2 classes** — matching the MOY/adjunction sector structure, with the reduced-part comparison to OS08 Thm 5.6 flagged by them as future work. ## 3. What this does to the census-dependent superstructure

**FR-1 resolves by relocation.** The Jabuka–Mark contradiction was real and fatal — *for the wrong manifold*. On the carrier there is no k = 0 sector of that shape; the "rank 6" question is void rather than repaired; allocation A and D-alloc-H as stated are **retired, not fixed** (they were hypotheses about Σ_g×S¹'s torsion sector). The g = 1 check is satisfied trivially (the manifolds coincide there). What replaces the old hard-sector question: the middle class g−1 (reducible-only, spin-type, MOY-degenerate) — whose HF_red is part of OBTAIN-5.6's answer.

**The sector count changes: 2g−1 → 2g−2 (all torsion classes) or 2g−3 (irreducible-carrying classes)** — which of the two the framework's "protected sector" notion selects is a genuine question the old census never had to face (it is the reducibles-in-or-out question, physically: does the protected census count the flat/vacuum-adjacent content or only the vortex content?). **Every Diophantine identification downstream changes accordingly.** Corrected count-level menus [computed, `fr2_census_calc.py`; presented under the fresh Track W referee's standard — *"count-level matching is not evidence"* — as arithmetic with **zero evidential weight** pending module-level data]: with 2g−2 sectors, (p−1)(p′−1) = 4g−4: g = 2 gives {(2,5)} (Lee–Yang, non-unitary); first unitary entry (4,5) at g = 4.
