## Abstract

Several cosmological proposals posit that our universe has a CPT-conjugate partner, with the pair — not either member — forming the symmetric object. The operator-algebraic structure suited to such a pairing is well known: it is the **thermofield-double / standard-form structure** — partner = commutant, exchange = modular conjugation, joint state = cyclic-separating vector — written down for black holes by Israel (1976) and Maldacena (2001), identified with Tomita–Takesaki modular conjugation by Ojima (1981), analyzed as the algebraic locus of CPT by Swanson (2019), and realized for the two static patches of de Sitter space in the setting of Chandrasekaran–Longo–Penington–Witten (2023). This note does three limited things. First, it makes explicit the transcription of *CPT-paired cosmology as such* — the universe/anti-universe pair at whole-universe scope — into this structure, stating the substantive physical assumption that transcription requires (a cosmological standard form: the global observable algebra with nontrivial commutant and a cyclic-separating physical state), which is a postulate, not a theorem. Second, it records the structural consequences that then follow: the partner carries no independent initial data; the restriction to one member admits no pure-state description; and the partner's modular data are inverse to ours — a relative-sign statement, not by itself an arrow of time. Third, it poses what we believe is an open problem: exhibit the CPT involution of a concrete CPT-symmetric cosmology (e.g. Boyle–Turok's) as the modular conjugation of an explicitly constructed standard form, including a prescription for the algebra at the involution's fixed locus. The contribution is a synthesis and a posed problem; no new mathematics is claimed.

## 1. The inputs, all standard

Let M be a von Neumann algebra acting on H, with a vector Ω cyclic and separating for M. Tomita–Takesaki theory [Takesaki 1970; Bratteli–Robinson I §2.5] gives S = JΔ^{1/2} (closure of AΩ ↦ A*Ω), with J antiunitary involutive and Δ positive, satisfying:

1. **JMJ = M′**;
2. **Δ^{it} M Δ^{−it} = M** — σ_t = Ad Δ^{it} is the modular automorphism group of (M, Ω);
3. **ω = ⟨Ω, · Ω⟩ is a KMS state on M for σ_t** — we fix the Bratteli–Robinson convention, in which ω is (σ, −1)-KMS, equivalently KMS at inverse temperature β = 1 for the flow t ↦ σ_{−t}; all sign-dependent statements below are made in this convention;
4. **the standard form of the commutant:** using JΔ^{it}J = Δ^{it}, the modular objects of the pair (M′, Ω) are J′ = J and **Δ′ = Δ^{−1}**; hence the modular flow of M′ *in its own standard form* is t ↦ Ad Δ^{−it}. There is one global unitary flow on H; "the partner runs oppositely" means exactly and only that the commutant's own modular data invert the sign of the generator — a relative-sign statement.

Two anchors from QFT. The Bisognano–Wichmann theorem [1975/76] identifies, for the wedge algebra of a finite-component Wightman theory in its vacuum sector, Δ^{it} with the boost and J with the CRT operator composed with a rotation. Stated with care: this is a *theorem* for finite-component Wightman fields; in the purely algebraic setting the corresponding property (modular covariance) is an additional condition, and counterexamples among generalized free fields exist [Yngvason]. The symmetry delivered at theorem level is **CRT**; "CPT" requires composing with a rotation and is dimension-sensitive — we keep the distinction explicit and use "CPT-type" for the antiunitary conjugation at issue. Borchers' theorem [1992] supplies the algebraic converse anchor: positive-energy half-sided translations plus modular data force the commutation relations that make J act as a reflection on the translations — modular conjugation carries reflection-type spacetime meaning wherever those hypotheses hold. Second anchor: for *local* (wedge/double-cone) algebras of reasonable QFTs the factors are hyperfinite type III₁ [Buchholz–D'Antoni–Fredenhagen; Haagerup], for which normal states restrict thermally — a statement about local algebras, used below only where tagged.

## 2. The transcription, and the assumption it costs

A CPT-symmetric cosmology asserts: *(i)* reality comes as a pair (universe, conjugate universe); *(ii)* an antiunitary CPT-type map exchanges the members; *(iii)* the pair, not the member, carries the symmetric data. The transcription:

> **Universe = M; conjugate universe = M′; the exchange = J; the symmetric datum of the pair = Ω.** [interpretation]

**The assumption, stated as such (A1 — the cosmological standard form).** Irreducibility is a property of a *representation*, not of the abstract algebra: in the GNS representation of a pure global state the commutant is trivial and the transcription above is empty, while in the GNS representation of a faithful normal state the commutant is automatically nontrivial — that is the thermofield-double construction itself. A1 is therefore precisely: **the physically realized representation of the universe's global observable algebra is standard — the GNS representation of a faithful normal state — rather than irreducible.** This is the two-member ontology in algebraic form — a postulate matching the CPT-cosmology it transcribes, not a derivation of it; and in the gravitational setting, current work on algebras of closed universes (the crossed-product line descending from CLPW) puts independent pressure on nontrivial whole-universe algebras. We flag A1 as the note's substantive physical assumption. [assumption, load-bearing]

**Where this structure already lives.** The commutant-pair structure is the **thermofield double**: Israel (1976) for black holes; Maldacena (hep-th/0106112), where the second copy is the commutant and the state the shared cyclic-separating vector; **Ojima (Ann. Phys. 137 (1981) 1), where the TFD tilde-conjugation is identified with Tomita–Takesaki's J** — the modular-conjugation reading is forty years old; **Swanson (SHPMP 2019)**, a dedicated analysis of the CPT–modular-conjugation nexus; and in cosmology it is the setting of **CLPW (arXiv:2206.10780)** — the two static patches of de Sitter as mutual commutants with the shared state's modular structure thermal on each (the QFT-level frame, in the Gibbons–Hawking/Sewell line, on which their crossed-product construction builds; we use the frame, not their gravitational result). There is, further, an active recent literature treating two-sheet and mirror universes in TFD terms with CRT as a gauge symmetry [Cheng arXiv:2411.11056; Banks arXiv:2607.03199; arXiv:2603.12434], which any reader of this note should have in hand. **The nearest prior art, engaged exactly: Cheng writes, for two-sheet de Sitter universes and citing Boyle–Finn–Turok, a density-matrix relation ρ_CRT = e^{−πK}Θ_CΘ_RΘ_T — which is the modular relation S = JΔ^{1/2} in physicist's clothing. What Cheng does: apply the TFD/CRT structure to a two-sheet cosmology at the level of states and involutions. What Cheng does not do: state the Tomita–Takesaki identification (partner = commutant, exchange = modular conjugation, at whole-universe scope) or pose the construction problem of §4. That boundary is the boundary of this note's residual content, and we place it in the reader's view deliberately.**

**What is left for this note, exactly.** Against that literature, the residual content is: **(α)** the explicit statement that the *CPT-paired-cosmology ontology at whole-universe scope* is the standard-form structure under assumption A1 — the identification is implicit in the TFD/CRT literature but, to our (previously corrected, now re-scoped) searches, not stated for the universe/anti-universe pair as such; and **(β)** the bridge problem of §4, for which we found no prior statement. Both claims are bibliographic and falsifiable; we invite correction. [conjecture — bibliographic, scoped]

## 3. Three structural consequences [each with its honest strength]

**(a) The partner carries no independent initial data.** Given (M, Ω), the commutant and every expectation value in it are determined; there is no second state to tune. Honestly put: this does not *dissolve* the two-copy fine-tuning question — it relocates it into assumption A1 (why a standard form with this Ω?). What it removes is only the freedom for the partner to differ from the mirror image. [derived from §1.1, given A1]

**(b) Restriction to one member admits no pure-state description.** The state ω restricted to M is KMS for the modular flow — but this is a property of *every* faithful normal state and so, by itself, predicts nothing; we do not repeat the "inaccessibility = temperature" claim in that form. What survives without further input: Ω is separating, so no member-restricted observer sees a vector state of their own algebra as pure in the naive sense; and *if* the modular flow is granted independent physical meaning (the thermal-time hypothesis [Connes–Rovelli] — an import with its own open problems, not made here), the restriction is thermal in the physical sense, with type III₁ [local-algebra tag, §1] then excluding any purification internal to the member. [structure, honestly bounded; the stronger thermal reading is conditional on thermal time]

**(c) The relative sign of the partner's modular data is a theorem.** By §1.4, the commutant's own modular flow is generated by −log Δ where ours is generated by +log Δ. *If* modular flow grounds a dynamical arrow (again thermal-time territory, not claimed here), the partner's arrow is opposite by theorem rather than stipulation; without that import, what stands is the inverse-flow statement itself — which is precisely the ingredient CPT-cosmologies stipulate and the standard form supplies for free. [derived; the arrow reading conditional, and stated as such in the abstract as well]

## 4. The bridge problem

Boyle–Turok's CPT-symmetric universe pairs two temporal sheets across the bang; the pairing lives in the extended solution. The algebraic form above is kinematic — no bang, no metric continuation. The two are candidate faces of one structure, and the bridge is a problem we can state precisely:

> **Exhibit a concrete cosmological model in which the Boyle–Turok pairing involution is the modular conjugation J of an explicitly constructed standard form (M, Ω) — the analogue, for the temporal pair, of what the static-patch exchange is for de Sitter in the CLPW frame — including a prescription for the observable algebra at the involution's fixed locus.** Precision on *which* involution (a correction from refereeing): Boyle–Turok's symmetry package contains both the **sheet-exchange/time-reflection isometry**, which fixes the bang surface pointwise, and the **full CPT map**, which composes it with C and P and has a strictly smaller spacetime fixed set. The candidate for J — an antiunitary algebra-to-commutant map — is the **full CPT-type involution**; the fixed-locus clause then has two layers: the geometric fixed set of the underlying isometry (the bang surface, where local algebras degenerate and the extended solution's analyticity is itself contested) and the algebraic requirement M ∩ M′ = center (trivial for factors). The problem is well-posed only together with a statement of what happens to the algebra on the bang surface and of how the internal (C) part of the involution acts there. [open problem; the note's payload]

## 5. What this note does not claim

No cosmological model, no equation of state, no perturbation spectrum, no prediction, no new mathematics. Under assumption A1 — stated as an assumption — the CPT-paired ontology is the standard-form/thermofield-double structure, with consequences (a)–(c) at the strengths individually tagged; the synthesis is aimed where the TFD and CRT-cosmology literatures have not aimed it; and the bridge problem, with its fixed-locus clause, is posed for whoever can solve it. The scoped bibliographic claims of §2 are falsifiable and their falsification would be welcome information.
