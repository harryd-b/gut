## Abstract

Let Y(g,n) be the circle bundle of Euler number n > 0 over a closed oriented surface of genus g ≥ 2. We record two elementary observations. **(1)** The integral triple cup product form on H¹(Y(g,n);ℤ) vanishes identically; hence HF^∞(Y(g,n), 𝔰) is *standard* — Λ*(H¹(Y;ℤ)⊗𝔽) ⊗ 𝔽[U,U⁻¹] over 𝔽 (Lidman), and over ℤ by Levine–Ruberman — for every torsion spin^c structure, despite b₁ = 2g ≥ 4. This contrasts with the trivial bundle Σ_g×S¹, whose torsion sector has nonstandard HF^∞ and integral 2-torsion (Jabuka–Mark). **(2)** From Ozsváth–Szabó's Theorem 5.6, HF⁺_red(Y(g,n); 𝔽) = 0 for all n ≥ 2g−1 (recorded for completeness; this vanishing is already stated and used by Mark–Tosun), while at the extremal value n = 2g−2 — up to orientation the unit tangent bundle of Σ_g, i.e. Γ\PSL(2,ℝ) for hyperbolic surfaces — the reduced homology has **total rank one**, concentrated in the unique self-conjugate class forced by conjugation invariance, which on the Seiberg–Witten side is the vortex-free spin-type class of Mrowka–Ozsváth–Yu. Thus the unit tangent bundle is extremal among circle bundles: the largest Euler number carrying any reduced Floer homology, and it carries exactly one class — an observation we have not found recorded, sitting precisely on the Milnor–Wood line, where horizontal foliations end and Fuchsian rigidity begins (§4). We triangulate against the Seiberg–Witten moduli picture and the embedded contact homology of prequantization bundles (Nelson–Weiler), and pose the d-invariant table for all torsion classes as the natural next computation.

## 1. Setup and notation

Y(g,n) denotes the total space of the circle bundle of Euler number n > 0 over Σ_g, g ≥ 2. Standard Gysin-sequence facts: b₁(Y(g,n)) = 2g; H²(Y(g,n);ℤ) ≅ ℤ^{2g} ⊕ ℤ/n, with the torsion generated by the image of π*: H²(Σ;ℤ) → H²(Y;ℤ); spin^c structures with torsion c₁ form a torsor over ℤ/n. By the adjunction inequality applied to the vertical tori (Ozsváth–Szabó, *Properties and applications*, Thm 1.6, with genus-one surfaces), HF⁺ vanishes in every non-torsion spin^c structure; all content lies over the ℤ/n of torsion classes. Orientation reversal identifies Y(g,−n) with −Y(g,n) and dualizes the Floer homology in the standard way, so n > 0 is no loss. For n = 2g−2, Y(g, 2g−2) is (up to orientation convention) the unit tangent bundle T¹Σ_g; for hyperbolic Σ_g this is the homogeneous space Γ\PSL(2,ℝ), carrier of the geodesic and horocycle flows — which is the geometric reason this particular Euler number deserves a name.

## 2. Remark 1: the triple cup form vanishes; HF^∞ is standard

**Proposition 1.** *For n ≠ 0, the integral triple cup product form μ_Y: Λ³H¹(Y(g,n);ℤ) → ℤ vanishes identically. Consequently, for every torsion spin^c structure 𝔰, HF^∞(Y(g,n), 𝔰) is **standard** — over 𝔽 (Lidman [L]): ≅ Λ*(H¹(Y;ℤ) ⊗ 𝔽) ⊗ 𝔽[U,U⁻¹] ≅ Λ*(𝔽^{2g}) ⊗ 𝔽[U,U⁻¹]; and over ℤ (Levine–Ruberman [LR, Thm 3.2]) — despite b₁ = 2g.*

*Proof.* H¹(Y;ℤ) = π*H¹(Σ;ℤ) (Gysin, n ≠ 0), so any triple product of degree-one classes is π* of a class in H³(Σ;ℤ) = 0. (Alternatively: α∪β lands in the torsion subgroup of H²(Y), and H³(Y;ℤ) ≅ ℤ is torsion-free.) The standardness over 𝔽 is Lidman's theorem [L, Thm 1.1] (torsion 𝔰: HF^∞ is the homology of Λ*(free part of H¹) ⊗ 𝔽[U,U⁻¹] with differential ι_{μ_Y}; zero form, zero differential); over ℤ it is Levine–Ruberman [LR, Thm 3.2] (vanishing triple cup form ⟹ HF^∞ standard, integrally). ∎

**Caution on the group (a correction from refereeing this note):** "standard" means the exterior algebra on the *free part* H¹(Y;ℤ) ⊗ 𝔽 ≅ 𝔽^{2g}. For even n — including n = 2g−2 — the group Λ*H¹(Y;𝔽) would be wrong: H¹(Y;𝔽) has dimension 2g+1 by universal coefficients (the ℤ/n contributes), and using it overstates the rank by a factor of 2. The contrast with n = 0 remains stark: on Σ_g×S¹ the triple form is nonzero, HF^∞ in the torsion sector is nonstandard of 𝔽-dimension 2^{2g−1} + 2^{g−1} per degree, with integral 2-torsion for g ≥ 3 [JM, Thm 1.2, Cor 1.3]. Among circle bundles over Σ_g, the *trivial* one is the pathological one.

## 3. Remark 2: extremality of the unit tangent bundle

The input is Ozsváth–Szabó's Theorem 5.6 [OS, §5.2]: for [i] ∈ ℤ/n, with j the integer of minimal absolute value congruent to i mod n,

> HF⁺_red,∗(Y(g,n), i; 𝔽) ≅ ⊕_{s ≡ i (n), s ≠ j} X_{c(i,s)}(g, g−1−|s|),

where X(g,d) = ⊕_{k=0}^{d} Λ^{2g−k}H¹(Σ) ⊗ ℤ[U]/U^{d−k+1} (so H_*(Sym^d Σ) ≅ X_{*−2d}(g,d), and X(g,d) = 0 for d < 0), and c(i,s), built from the lens-space d-invariant d(n,i), is as in [OS].

**Proposition 2.** *(a) If n ≥ 2g−1, then HF⁺_red(Y(g,n), i; 𝔽) = 0 for every i. — This is recorded for completeness with its one-line proof; it is not new: it is stated and used in Mark–Tosun [MT] (via the same Theorem 5.6), and the large-n regime underlies the circle-bundle arguments of [OSz-2003, §9.4]. (b) If n = 2g−2, then HF⁺_red(Y(g,n); 𝔽) has total dimension 1: it vanishes in every torsion class except the class [g−1] of the two window endpoints s = ±(g−1), where it is 𝔽, generated by X(g,0) = Λ^{2g}H¹, in the grading c determined by [OS] (for i = g−1, c = d(2g−2, g−1) − 1 + (g−1); numerically (4g−9)/4 in the normalization of [OS] eq. (2)). The surviving class is necessarily **self-conjugate** — conjugation invariance of HF⁺ plus total rank one forces it, independent of labeling conventions — and on the Seiberg–Witten side it is the vortex-free class with the degenerate spin-type reducible ([MOY, Cor 5.8.5]: E ≅ π*K^{1/2}). **Orientation note:** the unit tangent bundle T¹Σ_g has Euler number 2−2g and is −Y(g, 2g−2); the total-rank statement is orientation-insensitive over 𝔽, but the grading (4g−9)/4 is orientation-sensitive and belongs to Y(g, 2g−2) (= ST\*Σ_g with the boundary orientation of the disk bundle).*

*Proof.* Since X(g, g−1−|s|) = 0 unless |s| ≤ g−1, only s in the window W = [−(g−1), g−1] contribute; W contains 2g−1 integers. (a) If n ≥ 2g−1, each residue class mod n meets W in at most one integer s; and if it does, then |j| ≤ |s| ≤ g−1 forces j ∈ W, hence j = s by uniqueness — so every window representative is excluded and the sum is empty. (b) If n = 2g−2, every nonzero class except [g−1] meets W exactly once (again necessarily at j), and [g−1] meets it exactly twice, at s = ±(g−1), which are congruent mod 2g−2 and tie for minimal absolute value; whichever is chosen as j, the other contributes X(g, g−1−(g−1)) = X(g,0), of dimension 1. That the class of ±(g−1) is the spin-type class matches the Seiberg–Witten side, below. The tie in j affects only the internal labeling, not the statement. ∎

**Consistency triangulation.** (i) On the Seiberg–Witten side, Mrowka–Ozsváth–Yu [MOY, Cor 1.0.5, Thm 5.9.1] identify the moduli on Y(g,n) as one Jacobian torus of reducibles per torsion class plus vortex components C±(e) ≅ Sym^e(Σ), 0 ≤ e ≤ g−2, in classes ±e — so the class [g−1] at n = 2g−2 is precisely the vortex-free class (and the Morse–Bott-degenerate spin-type case of [MOY, Cor 5.8.5]). The surviving reduced class lives exactly where there are no vortices to cancel; [OS]'s own remark attributes the rank drop with n to "flow-lines which connect the vortex moduli spaces with the Jacobian torus." (ii) On the contact side, Nelson–Weiler [NW, Thm 1.1] compute ECH of these prequantization bundles per torsion class, with all 2g−2 classes nonzero — the towers of the standard HF^∞ of Remark 1, consistently. (iii) At n = 1 the formula reproduces [OS, Thm 5.5]; we have verified the assembled statements by machine for g ≤ 6.

**The extremal reading.** Among circle bundles over Σ_g with n > 0, the reduced Floer homology is rich for small n, dies entirely for n ≥ 2g−1, and at the last nonvanishing value n = 2g−2 consists of exactly one class in exactly the spin-type sector. The manifold at that extremal value is the unit tangent bundle — the phase space of the surface's geodesic flow. We do not know a prior statement of this extremality, and we would welcome a pointer if one exists; the ingredients are all in [OS] and [MOY], and the observation may simply have not been worth anyone's page space before. We think the geometric distinction of the extremal manifold makes it worth a page.

## 4. Questions

(1) An integral lift of Proposition 2's part (b) (Thm 5.6 is over 𝔽; note [LR, Thm 3.2] already supplies the integral standardness of HF^∞ here, removing the [JM]-type torsion mechanism — this question is now much softer than in revision 1). (2) **The d-invariant table** — absolute gradings/correction terms in all torsion classes of all Y(g,n), now accessible via [LR]'s generalized correction terms since HF^∞ is standard integrally: to our knowledge this table is not in the literature, and computing it would upgrade this note from remarks to a small paper. We register it as our declared next step. (3) The ECH analogue of the reduced-part computation (flagged as future work in [NW]'s introduction) would make the triangulation of §3 a theorem-to-theorem match. (4) **The extremality has a classical skeleton we initially missed (we owe the observation to a referee): n = 2g−2 is the Milnor–Wood line** — horizontal foliations exist iff |e| ≤ 2g−2 (Wood), transverse contact structures iff e ≤ 2g−2 (Giroux, math/9911235), and at equality the horizontal structure is essentially unique and rigid (Goldman: maximal representations are Fuchsian; Matsumoto–Ghys). This suggests the conjectural dictionary: *the last reduced Floer class of circle bundles over Σ_g is the Fuchsian class — it survives because maximal = rigid* — with a plausible proof route: identify the surviving generator with the (twisted) Ozsváth–Szabó contact/Eliashberg–Thurston invariant of the perturbed Fuchsian (weak-stable) foliation. Caveat (also the referee's): HF⁺_red = 0 for n ≥ 2g−1 concerns *horizontal* structures, not tautness — those bundles still carry taut foliations (Gabai), whose detection needs twisted coefficients. We would be glad to see someone in contact topology write this; it is theirs more than ours.
