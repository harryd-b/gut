## 1. The Gleason cluster (phase 32)

**A2 — generalized Gleason: CONFIRMED.** Bunce–Wright covers *all* von Neumann algebras with no I₂ summand (type III included); uniqueness of the extension is explicitly asserted; completely additive ⇔ normal is Kadison–Ringrose II Thm 7.1.12. Cite the announcement (Bull. AMS 26 (1992) 288 = arXiv:math/9204228) *plus* the full-proof papers (J. London Math. Soc. 1994; Pacific J. Math. 172 (1996) 41). Positivity of the extension (state, not just functional) is standard and is the form in Hamhalter Ch. 5 / Rédei–Summers Prop. 4.2. For non-σ-finite algebras, "completely additive" (not merely σ-additive) is the right normality hypothesis — phase 32's wording already correct.

**A4 — simplicity of type III factors: CORRECTED, and the argument improved.** "A type III factor is a simple C*-algebra" is false in general: simplicity holds **iff countably decomposable (σ-finite)** (Blackadar, *Operator Algebras* 2006, Prop. III.1.7.11). σ-finiteness is harmless for separable-predual physics but must be stated. **Better: adopt the hypothesis-free route** — a character is in particular a tracial state, and *no properly infinite von Neumann algebra admits a tracial state* (1 = p + q with p ~ q ~ 1 forces τ(1) = 2τ(1)). This yields "no dispersion-free states" for every type III algebra, σ-finite or not, with no simplicity input. Phase 32 §5.1 should be rewritten on this route.

**A5 — generalized Kochen–Specker: CONFIRMED in substance, hypothesis sharpened.** The correct statement requires the no-I₂ hypothesis: *for N with no type I₂ summand*, two-valued finitely additive measures on P(N) exist iff N has a nonzero abelian direct summand (without no-I₂ it is false — M₂(ℂ) admits abundant two-valued measures). Openly checkable sources: Hamhalter, Ann. Inst. H. Poincaré 58 (1993) 173; Döring, IJTP 44 (2005) 139, Lemma 16 ff. Hamhalter-2003 theorem numbers: paywalled, [not verifiable online].

**A6 — pure normal states: CONFIRMED for factors** (the use made). General statement corrected for the record: type I is necessary but not sufficient (L^∞[0,1] has none); the sharp form is "pure normal state ⟺ atomic/minimal projection exists." Citation: Halvorson–Müger, Handbook of the Phil. of Physics (2007) p. 751.

**A3 — moot,** as anticipated: the type III component is a summand with no type I part by type decomposition; no factoriality needed for the I₂ exclusion.

## 2. The foliation cluster (phases 32 §1, 36 Layers 1–2)

**A1 — "GV ≠ 0 ⟹ type III component (Hurder–Katok)": CORRECTED in attribution and strength; the use survives via a corollary.** The actual theorem is **Connes', Theorem 0.3** of *Cyclic cohomology and the transverse fundamental class of a foliation* (Pitman RN 123, 1986; also NCG book, Ch. III): for a compact oriented, transversally oriented 3-manifold with codim-1 C² foliation, **GV ≠ 0 ⟹ the flow of weights mod(M) admits a finite invariant probability measure.** Hurder–Katok (Ann. Math. 126 (1987) 221) supply the Godbillon-measure/ergodic machinery, not the operator-algebra conclusion. The program's use is saved by the corollary: a semifinite algebra's flow of weights is translation on ℝ, which admits no invariant probability — so **GV ≠ 0 does force a nontrivial type III component** [corollary of Connes 0.3; safe]. But two things the record has leaned on are **not established anywhere**: (i) **factoriality** of W*(S³,F) for Thurston-type foliations (needs ergodicity of the leaf equivalence relation — no source asserts it); (ii) the **subtype III₁** (AMK assert it; Connes' theorem is consistent with III₁, III_λ, and special III₀, and decides nothing among them). **Downstream:** M0b/M0c stand (they need only "type III component, no I₂"); phase 32 §5.4 (Connes–Størmer homogeneity) and every "outer at *every* t" statement are **III₁-conditional and now flagged as resting on an unproven subtype**. The one remaining genuinely open operator-algebra question of the program is exactly this: *factoriality and subtype of the Thurston-foliation algebra* — a sharp, publishable question for a foliation operator-algebraist.

**E1 — converse landscape: RESOLVED.** The converse "type III ⟹ GV ≠ 0" is **false**. GV = 0 is compatible with type III: the archetypal type III (indeed III₁) foliation algebras — non-unimodular group actions, Anosov weak-stable foliations — carry no forced GV. Duminy (1982, unpublished; exposition Ghys, Sém. Bourbaki 706; Cantwell–Conlon): GV ≠ 0 ⟹ resilient leaf, so foliations without resilient leaves have GV = 0 yet can lack invariant transverse measures. Thurston's realization (Bull. AMS 78 (1972) 511): GV takes every real value on foliations of S³ — confirmed. **Physics consequence:** phase 36 Layer 1's caveat is confirmed and sharpened — the fold is *an* activator of intrinsic time, demonstrably not *the* activator; the program's conjecture that it is the *physical* one is now cleanly separated from any mathematical necessity.

**E2 — the Connes pairing: CONFIRMED verbatim; one formulation retired for good.** Connes' own displayed relations match phase 36 Layer 2 exactly: [Ẋ] = d/dt σ_t[X], **d²/dt² σ_t[X] = 0**, **GV = i_H[Ẋ]** (contraction of the time-evolution generator with the first time-derivative of the transverse fundamental class), plus the GV cyclic 2-cocycle on C*(V,F) (which, by Thurston's arbitrary-real-values result, does *not* come from KK — a genuinely secondary invariant). Moriyoshi–Natsume (Pacific J. Math. 172 (1996)) give the analytic realization (GV index theorem) [substance verified; verbatim paywalled]. **However, "the flow of weights is determined by GV" — the phase 25 formulation — overstates Connes and is now definitively retired:** the theorem *constrains* (invariant probability exists), it does not *determine*. Phase 36's "metered" reading is the correct one and is hereby confirmed as such. The interpretive layer (σ_t as physical time, thermodynamic readings) is the program's own, not Connes' — as the record already says.

## 3. The AQFT cluster (phase 33)

**B1 — local normality: CONFIRMED as a layered standard, citation corrected.** It is a *selection criterion* (Haag's book, Ch. III — not Haag–Kastler 1964) plus theorems for the relevant state classes: KMS states (Takesaki–Winnink, CMP 30 (1973)), massive particle sectors (Buchholz–Fredenhagen, CMP 84 (1982)), nuclearity/split (Buchholz–Wichmann, CMP 106 (1986)); curved-space analogue Verch, CMP 160 (1994). Quasi-equivalence upgrades automatically on σ-finite type III factors. **Circularity warning adopted into L1's statement:** "physically admissible" must be defined by a named state class or nuclearity condition, not by local normality itself.

**B2 — model-independent Bisognano–Wichmann: CONFIRMED with the decisive scope correction.** BW is **not free**: Borchers' theorem alone does not give it, and counterexamples exist (Yngvason, LMP 31 (1994); Longo–Morinelli–Rehren infinite-spin, CMP 345 (2016); Morinelli's massive modular-covariant counterexamples, AHP 19 (2018)). The licensing hypotheses, any one of which suffices: Wightman-generated net (Bisognano–Wichmann 1975/76); **Mund's theorem** (AHP 2 (2001) 907): d = 4, massive, asymptotically complete; **Dybalski–Morinelli** (CMP 380 (2020) 1267): massless asymptotically complete (photons covered); conformal covariance (Brunetti–Guido–Longo, CMP 156 (1993)); or modular covariance *assumed*. **M1's theorem statement must carry one of these as an explicit hypothesis** — the assembly stands, with its hypothesis list now honest and complete. Also adopted: the conflation warning — the canonical outer class δ (state-independent) and the *vacuum's* concrete wedge modular flow are different objects; BW is about the latter; L2 correctly relates them via the class, and the wording should keep them distinct.

**B3 — Connes cocycle: CONFIRMED with the precision phase 33 anticipated.** Cocycle theorem: Takesaki, *TOA II* §VIII.3; **inner ⟺ semifinite is Thm VIII.3.14** (exact number verified via Masuda's survey). The T-invariant refinement: type III ⟺ outer for *some* t; **III₁ ⟺ outer for every t ≠ 0** (T(M) = {0}); III_λ is inner at the discrete set (2π/|log λ|)ℤ. So L2's "one canonical outer flow, nontrivial at every t" is **III₁-specific** — correct under the program's assumption, but that assumption is exactly the unproven [A1] subtype. The audit closes B3 and re-opens it as A1's dependency, which is the honest bookkeeping.

**B4 — Kay–Wald + Sewell: CORRECTED wording.** Kay–Wald (Phys. Rep. 207 (1991) 49) give **at-most-one** (uniqueness on a specified subalgebra, for the linear/quasifree Klein–Gordon field, of the Killing-invariant Hadamard state) plus the KMS-at-κ/2π identity — **not existence**, which genuinely fails on Kerr (no stationary Hadamard state at all) and was only established for the static case by Sanders (2015). Sewell (Ann. Phys. 141 (1982) 201) *assumes* the BW-type geometric modular condition; he does not derive it. Phase 33's Hawking half must read: "temperature identity for the invariant Hadamard state *where it exists*, linear fields; Kerr excluded; interacting case an extrapolation."

## 4. The phase 38 items

**F1 — nonlinearity bounds: numerics CONFIRMED, scope CORRECTED — the cross-check is downgraded.** Bollinger et al., PRL 63 (1989) 1031: ε = 1.8(4.0) μHz·h, limit **|ε| < 2.4 × 10⁻²⁰ eV** (4 × 10⁻²⁷ of binding energy per nucleon); comparable: Walsworth H-maser 3.7 × 10⁻²⁰ eV, Majumder ²⁰¹Hg **1.6 × 10⁻²⁰ eV** (tightest). But **all these constrain internal-state (spin/hyperfine) nonlinearities only.** They do **not** transfer to nonlinearities coupling to spatial/which-path densities — the modern literature is explicit that bounds there are weak (Kaplan–Rajendran, PRD 105 (2022) 055002), and dedicated experiments were built for precisely that reason (Broz et al., PRL 130 (2023) 200201, trapped-ion motional mode). **Phase 38 §2's "framework-free cross-check" is accordingly downgraded from an exclusion to a conditional statement** (valid only under a universality assumption on the coupling). The **no-go verdict is unaffected** — it rests on the in-house κ = 0 (M0b), L1, and routes (i)–(iii) of phase 38 §3, none of which used F1.

**F2 — the equal-intensity null: RESOLVED against phase 38, by computation.** With a generic path-mixing free Hamiltonian H ≠ 0 added to the diagonal-density model, the equal-intensity configuration shows **I₃ = O(λ) with coefficient comparable to the generic case** (numerics, this session: I₃/λ ≈ 7.9 × 10⁻² at equal intensities vs 4.5 × 10⁻² generic; clean linear scaling over four decades). The exact null of phase 38 §1.3.2 is a **diagonal-model artifact**. **The experiment-design corollary ("Sinha-type equal-opening geometries are blind") is withdrawn**, surviving only for pure-dephasing implementations (no path mixing between the nonlinear region and detection). The no-go is marginally *strengthened*: there is no geometric blind spot to excuse existing null results. Script `p6_f2_calc.py` (Part 2) archived.
