## 0. The bet, in one sentence

There exists a four-dimensional metric-affine geometry $(M^4, e^a, \omega^a{}_b, T^a)$, ontologically fundamental, whose single generalized connection has total curvature decomposing into Riemann $\oplus$ torsion $\oplus$ Standard-Model gauge curvature; whose extra degrees of freedom come from the exotic differential-topological richness of 4-manifolds rather than from $D>4$; and on which the quantum Hilbert space and its entanglement structure are the operator-algebraic shadow (the AQFT net) of the geometry rather than its source.

**The planks (consolidated — Phase 11).** Originally four; Planks 1 and 3 are now merged (see note). The three planks:

- **Plank I** *(was 1 + 3 — the geometric-foundation plank)*: **the diffeomorphism type of a smooth 4-manifold is ontologically primitive, and dimension 4 is necessary, not incidental — because 4 is the unique dimension where the smooth structure is rich enough (exotic smoothness) to carry the physical content others outsource to extra dimensions or a pre-geometric substrate.**
- **Plank II** *(was 2)*: entanglement / quantum structure is the operator-algebraic shadow of the geometry (AQFT net), not its source.
- **Plank III** *(was 4)*: the Standard-Model forces are (twisted) geometry — gauge structure from the geometric data, not independent.

**Note on the 1+3 merger.** They are not *logically* identical — "spacetime fundamental" (1) is compatible with a fundamental higher-dimensional manifold, so "exactly 4" is extra content. But the *interesting* part of Plank 3 (exotic smoothness as a physical reservoir) **presupposes** Plank 1 (an emergent spacetime has no primitive smooth structure to be rich) and then **supplies what Plank 1 lacked** — the reason the dimension is 4. The merged plank is self-justifying where neither was alone, and Result E (Phase 6: exotic smoothness *sources gravity*) is the direct evidence that the smooth structure is physically primitive.

**The two named obstructions on the table from the start:** Wigner (particles are Poincaré irreps; spin-1 ≠ spin-2) and Coleman–Mandula (spacetime ⊗ internal symmetry can't merge nontrivially).

## 2. Results settled

### Result A — Gauge fields are not intrinsic 4D torsion. *(Doc 1)*

**Question.** Can the 24-component torsion tensor encode a $U(1)$ (later $SU(2)$) gauge potential intrinsically in 4D?

**Answer: No, and the rock is identified.** Torsion splits irreducibly as trace vector (4) ⊕ axial vector (4) ⊕ pure tensor (16).
- The **tensor (16)** part — the piece originally hoped for — carries *no Lorentz vector irrep*, so it cannot even be an $A_\mu$. Wrong shape.
- The **trace vector (4)** has the right rep but the wrong gauge group: torsion transforms *homogeneously* (it is field-strength-like, not connection-like), and the only inhomogeneous gauge freedom in the geometry is local Lorentz $\mathfrak{so}(1,3)$, never an internal $U(1)$. This lands exactly on **Weyl's failed 1918 geometrization of EM** (its true gauge group is scale/dilatation, killed by Einstein's spectral-line objection).
- The **axial vector (4)** is dual to a 3-form → an axion-like *pseudoscalar*, not a photon.
- Backstops: torsion's Bianchi identity is $DT^a = R^a{}_b\wedge e^b$, not $dF=0$ (no homogeneous Maxwell eqns); and minimal-EC torsion doesn't propagate, while quadratic extensions are generically ghost/tachyon-ridden.

**Consequence for the bet:** Plank 4 cannot route gauge fields through Riemann–Cartan torsion as a potential. The only surviving door is *enlarge the structure group, not the dimension* — which forces the confrontation in Result B.

### Result B — The bet survives the no-go theorems by exactly one door, shared with Plank 2. *(Doc 2)*

**Generalization.** The torsion obstruction is not a $U(1)$ accident; it holds for $SU(2)$ too. The tempting $\mathfrak{su}(2)$ inside $\mathfrak{so}(1,3)_{\mathbb C}$ (Ashtekar self-dual half) is the *wrong* one — it's a spacetime symmetry not commuting with boosts; using it would force weak-doublet partners $(\nu_e,e)$ to be Lorentz-related. Coleman–Mandula in miniature.

**Coleman–Mandula, assumption by assumption:**
- Drop Lorentz invariance → self-defeating (kills Plank 1). Reject.
- Drop mass gap (conformal) → only enlarges the *spacetime* factor; still a direct product; doesn't deliver the merger.
- **Drop the global S-matrix → the honest door**, justified by confinement (quarks aren't asymptotic states) + cosmology/background independence (de Sitter has no global S-matrix). This lands the program in **algebraic QFT in curved spacetime** — *the same framework Plank 2 already required.* **Planks 2 and 4 want the same escape hatch.** Genuine internal coherence.
- Cost: removing the S-matrix removes the tools too (particle concept, cross sections, the Wigner classification itself). The particle notion must be rebuilt from the local net. No realistic gauge unification has been built there.

**Weinberg–Witten (the sharper wall):** forbids emergent massless spin-$>1$ with a covariant stress tensor and emergent charged massless spin-$>\tfrac12$ with a covariant current. It forces two precisions: the **graviton stays fundamental** (= Plank 1; the loophole is GR's non-localizable energy/pseudotensor), and "unification" must mean gauge fields are **co-fundamental in one connection**, not bound states of the metric.

**Consequence for the bet:** the flag in §1. And a pointer: the place where dropping the S-matrix (AQFT) and "gauge group from geometry" actually meet rigorously is the **Doplicher–Roberts reconstruction theorem** → Doc 3.
