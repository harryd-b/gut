## Appendix — the constants (added 2026-07-17, evening; anticipating the obvious challenge)

**The framework's claim is that it has one constant — properly stated.** c, ħ, and k_B are not constants of nature here; they are conversion factors between the fold's natural units and SI (a position metrology itself adopted in 2019 by fixing all three by definition). In detail: **c** is the fold's space-to-time exchange rate (𝒯 = 2πR/c read backwards); its *value* is conventional, but its *universality* — one causal speed for all fields — is derived, conditionally, from the uniqueness of the canonical clock class (one clock ⟹ one causal structure). **ħ** appears only as the fold-to-energy conversion (R1: ħ = k_B T·𝒯 — identically 1 in fold units); the deep quantum facts are theorems (noncommutativity from holonomy; Born rule forced), and the old "value of ħ" residue resolved into a dimensionless, quantized deformation level — an integer, not a constant. **k_B** is pure convention. **G** is the nontrivial one: derived, not measured — 1/G = (signed species sum)/(12πR²) given the content — which is what promotes **R, the fold's curvature radius, to the framework's single genuine dimensionful constant**, with 𝒯, the fold temperature, and G all following from it.

**The full inventory, without overselling:** one length (R); a handful of integers (genus, deformation level, discrete content data); the derived scale relations — **and the ~25 dimensionless Standard Model parameters (α, mass ratios, mixing angles), which the framework does not derive and which hide inside the phrase "the particle content."** The honest form of the claim: *the framework has reduced its own foundations to one number and some integers, and has identified — in the dictionary problem — the single construction that would decide whether the remaining twenty-five are computable or forever inputs.*
