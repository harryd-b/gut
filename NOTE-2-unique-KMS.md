## Abstract

Let Γ be a cocompact Fuchsian group acting on its boundary circle, and consider the crossed product of C(S¹) (or L∞(S¹)) by Γ with the diagonal dynamics generated by the Radon–Nikodym (derivative) cocycle. Assembling two classical bodies of work — the correspondence between KMS_β states of such algebras and e^{−βc}-conformal quasi-invariant measures (Renault; Neshveyev; Christensen–Vaes), and Sullivan's theorem that a convex-cocompact group carries one and only one invariant conformal density, of dimension δ(Γ), the Hausdorff density — we record the resulting statement, which we have not found in the literature for Fuchsian boundary actions although its free-group avatar (via Cuntz–Krieger algebras) is classical: **the boundary algebra admits a unique KMS state; it occurs at the critical inverse temperature β = δ = 1; and it is the Patterson–Sullivan state, i.e. Lebesgue measure — whose associated geodesic-flow measure, by Sullivan's product formula, is the Bowen–Margulis measure, equal in this setting to Liouville measure.** We state the theorem with a complete proof-by-assembly, verify the free-group precedent, note the phase-transition reading (no KMS states below the critical temperature; the unique equilibrium exactly at it), and pose two questions: the extension to the type III₁ von Neumann setting as a uniqueness statement for the flow of weights' natural transversal data, and the geometrically finite case, where Roblin's uniqueness should extend the statement verbatim.

## 1. The two inputs

**(A) KMS ↔ conformal measures.** For an étale groupoid with dynamics from a continuous real cocycle c, KMS_β states of the reduced algebra correspond to quasi-invariant probability measures with Radon–Nikodym cocycle e^{−βc} (Renault, LNM 793, Prop. II.5.4); Neshveyev (J. Op. Theory 70 (2013)) removes principality (immaterial here: boundary actions of Fuchsian groups are topologically free); Christensen–Vaes (CMP 2022, Lemma 2.2) state the correspondence in exactly the crossed-product formalism used below. With c(γ, ξ) = log|γ′(ξ)| (the derivative cocycle in the round metric), a β-conformal measure is precisely a Γ-invariant conformal density of dimension β in Sullivan's sense.

**(B) Sullivan's uniqueness.** For convex-cocompact Γ (in particular cocompact, where the limit set is the whole circle and δ = 1): *"there is on Λ(Γ) one and only one Γ-invariant conformal density of any dimension, and this is the Hausdorff density of dimension δ(Γ)"* (Sullivan, IHÉS 50 (1979), Theorem 8; the divergence-type sharpening in Theorem 21; Roblin, Mém. SMF 95 (2003) for the general divergence-type statement). For cocompact Fuchsian groups the unique density is Lebesgue measure on S¹.

## 2. The theorem

> **Theorem.** Let Γ be a cocompact Fuchsian group and A = C(S¹) ⋊_r Γ with the one-parameter dynamics σ_t generated by the derivative cocycle. Then σ admits **exactly one KMS state, at β = 1**, given by the Patterson–Sullivan (= normalized Lebesgue) measure composed with the canonical conditional expectation. There are no KMS_β states for any β ≠ 1. The GNS von Neumann completion is the type III₁ boundary factor; the corresponding geodesic-flow measure (Sullivan's Prop. 11/Cor. 12: dμ dμ dt/|ξ−η|^{2δ}) is Bowen–Margulis = Liouville.

*Proof by assembly.* A KMS_β state yields (input A) a β-conformal density; Sullivan's Theorem 8 (input B) says such a density exists only at β = δ = 1 and is then unique; topological freeness kills isotropy contributions (Neshveyev's field of tracial states is trivial a.e.); conversely Lebesgue at β = 1 satisfies the conformality identity by inspection. The type and the BM/Liouville identifications are Sullivan §4 with the Hopf comparison, and the classical type III₁ computations for the boundary action. ∎

Remarks. (i) The free-group precedent: ∂F_n with C(∂F_n) ⋊ F_n ≅ a Cuntz–Krieger algebra, whose gauge action has a unique KMS state at β = log(2n−1) = δ — the same phenomenon, on print record since the 1980s; the Fuchsian statement appears to be folklore-by-assembly, unwritten. We would welcome correction. (ii) The phase-transition reading: the theorem is the zero-temperature-free, single-critical-point degenerate case of the Bost–Connes paradigm for boundary actions; the absence of any KMS state off criticality reflects the rigidity of the cocompact limit set. (iii) In the von Neumann setting the statement becomes: the dual weight of Lebesgue is, up to scaling, the unique KMS weight of its natural flow — the uniqueness underlying the "canonical state" of the boundary factor.

## 3. Two questions

(1) **Geometrically finite extension:** with Roblin's uniqueness replacing Theorem 8, the statement should extend verbatim to divergence-type groups with δ replacing 1 and the PS density replacing Lebesgue — including cusped Fuchsian groups; the KMS-at-δ-only phenomenon then interacts with the known entropy-rigidity landscape, and it would be worth recording whether finite covolume is needed for uniqueness of the *state* (as opposed to the density). (2) **Transversal formulations:** the same assembly through the Feldman–Moore/groupoid presentation on a transversal gives uniqueness statements for KMS weights of the foliation factor of the geodesic flow — a dictionary worth writing once, carefully, since the geodesic-flow avatar (Bowen–Margulis as unique equilibrium) is classical dynamics while the operator-algebraic phrasing appears unrecorded.
