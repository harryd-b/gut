## 2. The invisibility lemma, and the design it forces [derived, in-room]

> **Lemma (lift invisibility).** Two antilinear lifts of conjugation to the spinor bundle differ by a gauge transformation: j′ = u·j, u ∈ Map(Y, S¹), and (uj)² = −1 automatically. On the gauge quotient ℬ(Y,𝔰) the induced involution is therefore canonical. **Consequently any construction built downstream of ℬ(Y,𝔰) — Lin's, Hendricks–Manolescu's, anything of their shape — cannot depend on the spin lift.** The 2^{b₁} lifts form a torsor with no distinguished point, and quotienting erases it.

> **Corollary (the design principle).** A genuine spin refinement cannot be an "absolute" invariant assigned to a chosen lift — the choices are conjugate. It must instead be defined **at the fixed locus of the conjugation involution, where the torsor trivializes canonically:** the fixed points of A ↦ Ā on the flat torus H¹(Y;iℝ)/2πiH¹(Y;ℤ) are the 2-torsion points, and **each 2-torsion fixed point IS a flat real structure — canonically a spin structure, not a choice of one.** The refinement is not "pick a lift and compute" but "decompose the equivariant theory over the canonically labeled fixed points."
